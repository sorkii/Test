PERSONA

Research of student opinions about electricity companies, made through quantitative and qualitative questionnaires.

About

I created a persona based on qualitative and quantitative questionnaires in this project. The goal was to create a student persona and analyze this target group's opinion on general electricity companies and the made-up electricity company "Elbolaget AB".

My role: I was in charge of making a survey and creating an infographic for my persona.

Design Toolkit: Google Forms, Google Docs & Figma.

I also divided the survey into two parts, where the first part intended general perceptions about electricity companies. Meanwhile, the second part covered questions about specifically Elbolaget AB.

I published the questions in a Google Form sheet and sent out the form to eight students when the questions were completed. Once I received the responses, I could easily pick out the six areas to create my persona.

"Electricity contracts are boring but necessary"

- I received this quote from one of my respondents in the survey where they would describe their general perception of electricity contracts in a short sentence.

Results

The results showed that my persona is a student woman of 22 years. She makes environmentally conscious consumer choices, wants cheap electricity and doesn't like long queues for customer service.

I presented my persona to the stakeholder by addressing the insights of the six different areas. Then I also explained how I chose to formulate my questions for the survey and how I chose to divide the form.

Finally, I pointed out the importance of starting my persona in upcoming design processes for Elbolaget AB because it represents its users.

What have I learned from this project?

This project was very rewarding for me as I got to try both UX research and UI. The difficulty of the project was formulating questions that covered the six different topics of the persona template.

To solve this obstacle, I enlisted the help of my classmates and asked my course leader questions. Thus, today I have learned what kinds of questions I will use in future projects to get important information.
